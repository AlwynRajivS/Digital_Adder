Module 1: Why We Need Carries

Beginner

Adding numbers in binary follows the exact same logic as decimal, but you run out of digits much faster!

In decimal, 9 + 1 overflows one column, forcing you to write 0 and "borrow" 1 to the left: 10. In binary, the limit is 1. So 1 + 1 overflows immediately, yielding 0 and borrowing a 1, written as 102 (which equals 2 in decimal).

Module 2: The Half Subtractor

Core Logic

Gate Schematic

HIGH (1) LOW (0)

Module Explanation

A Half Subtractor is a combinational circuit that subtracts one binary bit from another. It generates a DIFFERENCE (using an XOR gate) and a BORROW (using an AND gate).

Limitation: It has no input pin for a borrow from a previous column. That's why it's called a Half Subtractor!

Intermediate

Circuit Schematic

Understanding Full Subtractors

A Full Subtractor adds three bits: inputs A, B, and a borrow-in bit Bin from a previous column. It outputs a DIFFERENCE and a new borrow-out Bout.

Look at the block view to see how we chain two Half Subtractors. The first adds A + B. The second adds the result to Bin. An OR gate captures if either subtractor generated a borrow.

Mapping to Real Chips

In a physical electronics lab, logic gates aren't individual components. Instead, you use Integrated Circuit (IC) chips, usually from the 7400-series TTL family.

  • 74LS86 (XOR): Contains 4 independent XOR gates. Pins 1 & 2 are inputs, Pin 3 is output for Gate 1. Pins 4 & 5 are inputs, Pin 6 is output for Gate 2.
  • 74LS08 (AND): Contains 4 independent AND gates. Pins 1 & 2 are inputs, Pin 3 is output for Gate 1. Pins 4 & 5 are inputs, Pin 6 is output for Gate 2.
  • 74LS32 (OR): Contains 4 independent OR gates. Pins 1 & 2 are inputs, Pin 3 is output for Gate 1.
  • Powering ICs: Every chip requires power connected to Pin 14 (VCC, +5V) and ground connected to Pin 7 (GND).
